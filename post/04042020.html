three themes

04.04.2020

CGPGrey, the youtuber, makes a point that abstract themes often lead to more personal growth than concrete goals. What does that mean? Instead of comitting to running five miles a day, or going to the gym 6 times a week, you will choose a theme of fitness. Your choices to act along this theme are much more flexible than just following one set goal.

During this shelter-in-place in California I have been working on a few themes to improve myself. They are fitness, food, and finding peace.

My fitness theme is fairly self explanatory. I have been running, and not taking more than two days off in a row as far as my workouts.

My food theme mostly involves cooking. I'm by no means a chef, so I still have to follow recipes, and sometimes I fail. I've been trying to master the more difficult techniques involved in making pastries, like macronage, or the endless lamination of croissant dough, with mixed results. One day I want to be at the point where I can create my own recipes, instead of following them, but I understand I must master the basic techniques first.

Finally, the theme of finding peace has largely manifested through mindfulness. Going through a trauamtic period of loss isn't easy for anyone, and I think my anxiety sometimes makes it worse. Nevertheless, I've been meditating for as long as I can stand every day, and letting myself have time to just sit and process the thoughts that come by.

Concrete goals and quantitative metrics have always had their place in my self-improvement journey, but I have been enjoying the flexibility of themes. Maybe they will eventually become too vague to be effective, but for now they are what I'm following.
